Does this really make any difference?

In case you are wondering, here is a bit of background.

Unlike some crowdfunding platforms, Indiegogo does not officially curate a list of campaigns that it deems cool, worthy or interesting. One of the key elements to obtaining backers on a platform such as Indiegogo is for the campaign to be seen as active and to be placed as high as possible in their 'Popular Now' list.

The Indiegogo platform ranks each campaign using a variety of criteria and a 'secret' algorithm - much like Google ranks internet pages. The higher a campaign scores, the better it is placed, with the best performers being placed on the Indiegogo landing page - generating the most traffic and potential for funding.

From our research Indiegogo base their ranking on criteria that includes:

  • funding (number of people who have pledged money, as well as amount of funding)
  • activity on a campaign's IGG page (including comments and referrals)
  • social media activity (facebook, twitter & google+ shares made through the indiegogo on-page share tools)
